Beowulf lines 1–11

Old English text

     Hwæt! Wē Gār-Dena      in geār-dagum
     þēod-cyninga      þrym gefrūnon,
     hū þā æðelingas      ellen fremedon.
     Oft Scyld Scēfing      sceaðena þrēatum,
     monegum mǣgðum      meodo-setla oftēah.
     Egsode eorl,      syððan ǣrest wearð
     fēa-sceaft funden:      hē þæs frōfre gebād,
     wēox under wolcnum,      weorð-myndum ðāh,
     oð þæt him ǣghwylc      þāra ymb-sittendra
     ofer hron-rāde      hȳran scolde,
     gomban gyldan:      þæt wæs gōd cyning!

Crude literal translation

Listen! We have heard of the glory of the kings of the Spear-Danes of olden days, how the princes showed courage.

Scyld Scefing often deprived enemy armies, many nations, of mead benches.

He terrified nobles after he at first came to be destitute found.

He therefore experienced comfort, grew under heaven, prospered with glory, until every neighboring people beyond the sea had to listen to him, pay tribute.

That was a good king!
